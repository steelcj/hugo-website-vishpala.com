## Principle

We build things that are meant to last. That commitment extends beyond the technical quality of the work to its environmental and operational footprint.

Sustainability at Vishpala means building systems that consume the minimum necessary resources, that can be maintained by small teams without specialised infrastructure, and that do not accumulate complexity faster than the capacity to understand and operate them.

## Digital sustainability

**Efficient infrastructure** — we design systems sized to actual workload. We do not recommend infrastructure at a scale that requires constant management overhead to justify its cost or complexity. Smaller, simpler, better-understood systems are almost always more sustainable than large, complex ones.

**Minimal dependencies** — every dependency is a liability: a thing that must be updated, monitored, and eventually replaced. We minimise dependencies as a matter of practice, preferring solutions that compose well from smaller, stable components over solutions that introduce large dependency trees.

**Long-lived software** — we prefer technologies with long track records and stable interfaces over technologies that require frequent rewrites to remain current. The most sustainable software is software that does not need to be replaced.

**No unnecessary data collection** — collecting data has an energy cost. Storing data has an energy cost. Transmitting data has an energy cost. We do not collect, store, or transmit data that does not serve a clear purpose.

## Operational sustainability

**Documentation** — undocumented systems are unsustainable systems. They accumulate technical debt invisibly and become dependent on the continued availability of the people who built them. We treat documentation as a core deliverable.

**Knowledge transfer** — we build with the intention that our clients can operate, maintain, and extend what we build without us. Dependency on an external consultant is not a sustainable operational model.

**Workload** — we do not take on more work than we can do well. Overextended practitioners produce lower quality work and burn out. We maintain a sustainable pace as a matter of professional principle.

## What we do not yet do

We do not currently measure or report the specific energy consumption of the infrastructure we operate or recommend. This is a gap we intend to address. We will update this commitment when we have a methodology we are confident in.
