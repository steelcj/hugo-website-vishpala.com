We practice design as a discipline of clarity. Our work begins with the people who will use it — their actual contexts, access levels, languages, and needs — and builds outward from there toward visual form, not the other way around.

Design at Vishpala is inseparable from accessibility. We treat WCAG 2.2 Level AA conformance as a floor, not a ceiling, and we test with real people across a range of abilities and assistive technologies at every stage of a project.

## What we work on

**Design systems** — token-based, component-level design infrastructure that can be maintained by a small team, translated without visual degradation, and extended without breaking existing patterns. We build for longevity, not for the current sprint.

**Accessible interfaces** — visual design, interaction design, and content design that work for people with a wide range of visual, motor, cognitive, and linguistic access needs. We conduct usability testing as a standard part of every project, not an optional extra.

**Multilingual design** — layouts and typographic systems that function correctly in French, English, and other languages without requiring separate design work per language. We design for the longest string, the right-to-left edge case, and the screen reader user simultaneously.

**Brand and visual identity** — identity systems for organisations that need to communicate consistently across contexts without a full-time design team. We deliver systems, not artefacts.

## Our approach

Good design is invisible to the person it serves. Our goal is never to produce work that announces itself as design. It is to produce work that allows the person using it to do what they came to do.
