We design and build infrastructure that organisations can own outright — not in the legal sense only, but in the operational sense. Systems they can understand, document, rebuild, and hand off without asking permission from a vendor.

Our infrastructure practice covers the full stack from physical hosting decisions through to deployment pipelines, monitoring, backup, and the documentation that makes it all maintainable by a human being at two in the morning.

## What we work on

**Deployment pipelines** — reproducible build and deployment systems that treat infrastructure as code, version-controlled and auditable. We prefer simple, composable tools over platforms that abstract away operational understanding.

**Self-hosted services** — migration away from proprietary SaaS platforms toward open, self-operated alternatives where this serves the organisation's sovereignty and privacy commitments. We do not advocate self-hosting for its own sake; we advocate it where it materially improves an organisation's agency.

**Operational documentation** — runbooks, architecture diagrams, and recovery procedures that reflect the actual system, not an idealised version of it. Documentation is treated as a first-class deliverable, not an afterthought.

**Resilience and recovery** — backup systems with tested restore procedures, incident response planning, and the kind of redundancy that matches the actual risk profile of the organisation rather than the risk profile of a much larger one.

## Our approach

We work from first principles rather than from a preferred tool stack. The right infrastructure for a community health clinic is not the right infrastructure for a national media organisation. We size the complexity of the solution to the operational capacity of the team that will maintain it.
