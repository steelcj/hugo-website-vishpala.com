Accessibility is not a checklist or a compliance exercise. It is the practice of building things that work for the full range of people who need to use them — across abilities, devices, languages, and connection speeds.

We approach accessibility as a discipline that runs through every stage of a project, not a phase that happens after design and development are complete. The cost of retrofitting accessibility is always higher than building it in from the start.

## What we work on

**Conformance audits** — systematic evaluation of digital products against WCAG 2.2 criteria, delivered as a prioritised remediation plan with clear ownership and testable acceptance criteria. We audit to AA as standard and AAA where the use case warrants it.

**Remediation** — hands-on remediation of accessibility issues in HTML, CSS, and content, including complex cases involving dynamic content, single-page applications, and custom interactive components.

**Procurement support** — accessibility requirements for RFPs and vendor evaluation criteria, including evaluation of VPAT documentation and independent testing of vendor claims.

**Organisational practice** — training, process integration, and tooling recommendations that build accessibility competence into a team's standard workflow rather than concentrating it in a single specialist.

**Usability testing** — moderated testing sessions with participants who use assistive technologies, screen readers, switch access, voice control, and other access methods, delivering qualitative findings that automated tools cannot surface.

## Our approach

Automated testing catches approximately 30% of accessibility issues. The rest require human judgment. We combine automated scanning with manual expert review and user testing because no single method is sufficient on its own.
